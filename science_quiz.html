Q 02 Astronomy

Which term defines a star system with two stars?

ABinary star system
BEclipse star system
COpen cluster star system
DGlobular cluster star system
Binary star system
A binary star system consists of two stars orbiting a common center of mass. "Binary" means two. Open and globular clusters refer to groups of many stars, and "eclipse star system" is not a real term.
Q 03 Earth Science

Which correctly lists the three types of rock particles that make up soil?

AAir, clay, and sand
BClay, water, and silt
CSand, clay, and silt
DSilt, water, and air
Sand, clay, and silt
Soil is composed of three types of rock (mineral) particles: sand (largest particles), silt (medium), and clay (smallest). Air and water are also found in soil but are not rock particles.
Q 04 Weather

Which process contributes to the formation of tornadoes?

AWater droplets freeze, giving off energy.
BThe storm begins to rotate around a central eye.
CThe Coriolis effect creates the spin of the storm.
DWarm air rises, resulting in a decrease in air pressure.
Warm air rises, resulting in a decrease in air pressure.
Tornadoes form when warm, moist air rises rapidly, creating low pressure near the surface. This upward motion combined with wind shear causes the air to rotate and eventually form a funnel cloud. A "central eye" is a hurricane feature, not a tornado's.
Q 05 Astronomy

What term describes the moon's path around Earth?

AEclipse
BPhase
CRevolution
DRotation
Revolution
Revolution is the orbital path one body takes around another. The Moon revolves around Earth roughly every 27.3 days. Rotation refers to spinning on an axis; phases are the Moon's appearance changes; an eclipse is when one body blocks another's light.
Q 06 Weather

ASea levels rising due to high waves
BCoastal flooding due to storm surge
CPaths of damage up to 50 miles long
DWind and rain lasting for several days
Paths of damage up to 50 miles long
Tornadoes are known for leaving narrow but intense damage paths that can extend up to 50 miles (or more). Storm surges, coastal flooding, and multi-day storms are characteristics of hurricanes, not tornadoes.
Q 07 Atmosphere

Which statement accurately describes the relationship between air pressure, air density, or altitude?

AAs altitude increases, pressure increases.
BAs altitude increases, air density increases.
CAir pressure and density are lowest at sea level.
DDenser air exerts more pressure than less dense air.
Denser air exerts more pressure than less dense air.
Air pressure is caused by the weight of air molecules. Denser air contains more molecules and therefore exerts greater pressure. At higher altitudes, air is less dense and pressure is lower — the opposite of options A and B.
Q 08 Weather

Which type of cloud forms close to the ground when the temperature is just above the dew point?

ACirrus
BCumulus
CFog
DStratus
Fog
Fog is essentially a cloud that forms at ground level when air temperature drops to just above the dew point, causing water vapor to condense near the surface. Cirrus clouds are high and icy; cumulus are puffy mid-level clouds; stratus are low, layered — but fog is closest to the ground.
Q 09 Physics / Light

Which statement best describes wavelengths of sunlight that are longer than 700 nanometers?

AThese wavelengths form the infrared part of the spectrum.
BThese wavelengths form the ultraviolet light of the spectrum.
CThese wavelengths are shorter than the wavelengths of visible light.
DThese wavelengths are part of the visible light spectrum.
These wavelengths form the infrared part of the spectrum.
Visible light spans roughly 380–700 nm. Wavelengths longer than 700 nm fall into the infrared region, which we feel as heat. Ultraviolet (UV) light is shorter than 380 nm, on the other side of visible light.
Q 10 Climate

How does altitude affect climate patterns in a region?

AAreas at high elevations have cooler temperatures.
BAir continues to warm as it rises to a higher elevation.
CTropical regions do not have many differences in altitude.
DHigh-altitude areas have less variation in seasonal temperatures.
Areas at high elevations have cooler temperatures.
Temperature decreases with altitude at about 3.5°F per 1,000 feet (environmental lapse rate). This is why mountain peaks are cold even in tropical areas. Air expands and cools as it rises, so higher elevations are consistently cooler.
Q 11 Earth History

A0

Earth has experienced five major mass extinction events (the "Big Five"): the Ordovician-Silurian, Late Devonian, Permian-Triassic (the largest, wiping out ~96% of species), Triassic-Jurassic, and Cretaceous-Paleogene (which ended the non-avian dinosaurs).
Q 12 Astronomy

What term specifically describes small chunks of rocks and debris in space that burn up in Earth's atmosphere?

AMeteorites
BMeteoroids
CMeteors
DMeteorides
Meteors
A meteoroid is a small rock in space. When it enters Earth's atmosphere and burns up (creating a streak of light), it becomes a meteor (a "shooting star"). If it survives and lands on Earth, it's called a meteorite. "Meteorides" is not a real term.
Q 13 Earth Science

A scientist is observing surface groundwater features to determine sources of groundwater. Which is one surface feature that the scientist can observe and map?

ASpring
BSaturated zone
CAquifer
DWater table
Spring
A spring is a place where groundwater naturally flows out to the surface — it's directly observable and mappable. The saturated zone, aquifer, and water table are all underground features that cannot be seen from the surface without drilling or instruments.
Q 14 Climate

Which describes one characteristic of both El Niño and La Niña?

ABoth are causes of long-term climate change.
BBoth create warmer water.
CBoth occur in the Pacific Ocean.
DBoth are causes of hurricane activity.
Both occur in the Pacific Ocean.
El Niño and La Niña are opposite phases of the El Niño-Southern Oscillation (ENSO), both centered in the tropical Pacific Ocean. El Niño warms Pacific waters while La Niña cools them — they are weather patterns, not causes of long-term climate change.
Q 15 Weather / Safety

Sara was caught in a thunderstorm while outdoors. She immediately makes sure that she is not near any tall structures or trees. What is Sara most likely trying to avoid?

ABeing struck by lightning
BBeing hit by thunder
CBeing caught by falling hail
DBeing carried away by strong winds
Being struck by lightning
Lightning tends to strike the tallest objects in an area. Trees and tall structures act as conductors. Standing near them greatly increases the risk of being struck. Thunder is sound and cannot physically harm someone; wind and hail are not specifically linked to tall objects.
Q 16 Earth Science

A scientist observes a geyser erupting. Which two objects must be interacting beneath the surface?

AWell and spring
BSpring and magma
CMagma and groundwater
DGroundwater and unsaturated zone
Magma and groundwater
A geyser erupts when groundwater is heated by magma deep underground, building pressure until steam and water shoot out through a vent. Without both heat (magma) and water (groundwater), no geyser can form.
Q 17 Astronomy

If astronomers discovered a new inner planet in the solar system, which characteristic would it most likely have?

ALarge size
BGaseous composition
CMany moons
DFast revolution
Fast revolution
Inner planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars) are smaller, rocky, and orbit close to the Sun — meaning they have shorter, faster orbital periods (Mercury takes only 88 days). Gas giants (Jupiter, Saturn, etc.) are large, gaseous, and have many moons, but those are outer planets.
Q 18 Space Exploration

Which tools are currently used to collect images during space exploration?

ARovers and satellites
BSatellites and rockets
CRockets and space shuttles
DSpace shuttles and rovers
Rovers and satellites
Rovers (like NASA's Curiosity and Perseverance on Mars) and satellites/space telescopes are actively used to capture images. Rockets are launch vehicles — they transport equipment but don't capture images themselves. The Space Shuttle program ended in 2011.
Q 19 Astronomy

What must happen for most areas on Earth to experience all four seasons?

AThe Sun must reach its highest point in the sky.
BThe Northern Hemisphere must experience winter.
CThe axis must be tilted at a certain angle that remains unchanged.
DThe north end of Earth's axis must always point away from the Sun.
The axis must be tilted at a certain angle that remains unchanged.
Earth's axial tilt of about 23.5° is what causes seasons. Because the tilt remains constant (pointing toward Polaris), different parts of Earth receive varying amounts of direct sunlight as Earth orbits the Sun throughout the year, producing spring, summer, fall, and winter.
Q 20 Astronomy

In December, a city in the Southern Hemisphere has warm weather all month long. What causes this to happen?

AThe city experiences fewer hours of daylight.
BThe city is experiencing a winter solstice.
CThere is a high concentration of the Sun's rays in that region.
DThe Sun reaches its highest point in the sky during this month.
There is a high concentration of the Sun's rays in that region.
In December, the Southern Hemisphere is tilted toward the Sun, so sunlight strikes at a more direct angle — creating a high concentration of solar energy and summer warmth. This is the opposite of the Northern Hemisphere, which experiences winter in December.
Q 21 Earth Science

Which term describes erosion?

ACreates small particles
BHardens rock fragments
CTransports solid materials
DDissolves rock components
Transports solid materials
Erosion is the process by which weathered rock and soil particles are transported (moved) by wind, water, ice, or gravity. Weathering creates small particles; chemical weathering can dissolve rock — but erosion specifically refers to the movement and carrying away of materials.
Q 22 Atmosphere

Which phrase defines altitude?

AHow tightly molecules are packed
BA column of air pushing on an area
CThe force pushing on an area or a surface
DThe height of a given point above sea level
The height of a given point above sea level
Altitude is defined as the height of a location above sea level. Air density describes how tightly molecules are packed; a column of air pushing down relates to air pressure. "Force on a surface" is the definition of pressure in general.

Q 24 Astronomy / Physics

Energy released by fusion in the Sun is initially in the form of...

AVisible light
BGamma rays
CUltraviolet light
DInfrared radiation
Gamma rays
Nuclear fusion in the Sun's core releases energy as extremely high-energy gamma rays. These photons take thousands of years to travel from the core to the surface, where they are re-emitted at lower energies (mostly as visible light and infrared) before reaching Earth.
✦ Bonus Questions
🌡️ What is the difference between weather and climate?
Weather is short-term; climate is the long-term average.
Weather refers to atmospheric conditions (temperature, rain, wind) over a short period. Climate describes the average weather patterns of a region over at least 30 years. "Climate is what you expect; weather is what you get."
🌍 What layer of Earth is responsible for the movement of tectonic plates?
The asthenosphere (upper mantle)
The asthenosphere is a semi-fluid layer of the upper mantle. Heat from Earth's interior creates convection currents here, which slowly move the rigid tectonic plates above it, causing earthquakes, volcanoes, and mountain building.
⭐ What are the stages of a star's life cycle (for a star like our Sun)?
Nebula → Protostar → Main Sequence → Red Giant → White Dwarf
Stars form from clouds of gas and dust (nebula). Gravity collapses the cloud into a protostar, which begins fusion and enters the long main sequence phase. It then expands into a red giant and eventually sheds its outer layers, leaving a dense white dwarf.
💧 What is the water cycle, and what are its four main stages?
Evaporation → Condensation → Precipitation → Collection
The water cycle is Earth's continuous movement of water. Evaporation turns liquid water into vapor; condensation forms clouds; precipitation brings water back as rain or snow; collection gathers water in oceans, lakes, and rivers — then the cycle repeats.
🌑 What causes a solar eclipse?
The Moon passes between Earth and the Sun, blocking sunlight.
A solar eclipse occurs when the Moon aligns directly between Earth and the Sun, casting a shadow on Earth. A total solar eclipse briefly blocks all direct sunlight. A lunar eclipse is the opposite — Earth's shadow falls on the Moon.
